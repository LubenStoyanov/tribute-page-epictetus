Epictetus

Once a slave, he became one of the most influential representatives of the late Stoa.

A timeline of Epictetus's life

  • Epictetus was born A.D. 50 at Hierapolis, Phrygia
  • His original name is unknown
  • epíktētos (ἐπίκτητος) means in Greek "gained" or "aquired"
  • In his youth he was a slave to Epaphroditos, a secretary to Nero
  • He studied stoic philosophy under Musonius Rufus and became more educated.
  • Under unknown circumstances he became crippled
  • After he obtained his freedom, he began to teach philosophy in Rome
  • Later he founded a philosophical school in Nicopolis in Epirus, Greece
  • His most famous pupil, Arrian, describes him as an powerful speaker
  • Eminent people wanted to talk with him.
  • He possessed little and lived a simple life. It's said that he didn't need a lock for his house, because there was nothing to steal
  • Most of the time he lived alone until he adopted a friend's child who would have died otherwise. A woman helped Epictetus to raise him properly. It's unkown if they were married
  • He died around 135 A.D.

"No man is free who is not master of himself.” – Epictetus
